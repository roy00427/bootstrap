Things to be done to protect Climate

Do's and Don't

Plantation

Planting trees is a cost-effective way to tackle air pollution which is a growing problem these days.

Deforestation

With the elimination of forests, the fertile soil flows through rain water to other places causing air pollution. .

Use of Bikes

Bicycling uses no fuel. Bikes don't require toxic batteries or motor oil. Cycling 10 km would save 1500 kg of greenhouse gas emissions each year and finally, prevent diseases.

Cars-global warming emissions

Transportation contributed more than half of the carbon monoxide and nitrogen oxides, and a quarter of the hydrocarbons emitted into air.

The Three R's

Reduce, Reuse, Recycle â€” Three great ways one can eliminate waste and protect our environment as well as resources.

Watage of Food

When food is discarded and broken down into landfills, it produces carbon dioxide and methane in the air.

Utilize Crop Residue for Soil and Energy

Crop residue can provide a protective layer for soil erosion by wind or water, can increase the organic matter and water holding capacity of the soil, provide food for worms and lastly it generates energy content .

Air Pollution due to stubble burning

Crop residue burning produces extremely high levels of toxic particulates, climate-smart reforms are needed to tackle severe air pollution that hits northern India every autumn, as a result of stubble burning.

Zero Emission Power Plants

Focus on installation of Zero Emission Turbine Plants as well as negative emission plants.

Industrial Pollution

Industries and Power plants contribute maximum amount of poisonous gases and particles to air.

Use Eco-friendly Material

Construction material should have low VOC emission, reusable substance should be used to reduce PM10,it is generated by dust, PM10-particulate matter less than 10 microns in diameter, invisible to the naked eye.

Dust Caused by Construction Sites

Construction activities produces dust typically from concrete, cement, wood, stone, silica and this can carry for large distances over a long period of time, which not only contribute to climate change but also penetrate deeply into the lungs.
